What is Valley Internal Medicine and Pediatrics?

As the name implies, an individual who completes a residency in Med/Peds is trained concurrently in both internal medicine and pediatrics. Thus, when the training is completed, the candidate is eligible to apply for certification in both internal medicine and pediatrics.

Internal medicine physicians have received specialized training to diagnose and treat complex medical problems in both adult and adolescent patients. Internists receive extensive training in detecting and managing complex diseases that may affect any organ system in the human body. They often serve the role of the “medical detective” as a consultant to other physicians or health care providers.

A pediatrician is a physician who treats the young patient in all aspects of health starting with early newborn well examinations and following their development until they become adults. Like the internist, the pediatrician is trained to detect and manage the complex problems that are unique to the younger patient. Most pediatricians will continue to follow their patients until they graduate from high school at which time their patients are transferred to the care of an “adult” physician.

Thus, the Med/Peds physician can take care of patients of all ages.

Dr. Mark Webb

Dr. Webb is a native of Detroit, Michigan. He moved to Arizona when his father relocated for business. He subsequently went to medical school in Tucson with residency in Phoenix for both internal medicine and pediatrics. Initially he worked for the Phoenix Indian Medical Center for 5 years before starting Valley Internal Medicine and Pediatrics in 1999. He has an interest in complex medical problems for all ages.

He has 3 grown children who reside presently in Arizona. He met his wife, Peggy, in medical school and she now practices as a clinical medical geneticist in town.

Dr. Susan Madsen

Dr. Madsen was born and raised in Chicago completing her medical school curriculum before moving to Wisconsin to complete her residency in both internal medicine and pediatrics. She did practice for 3 years before moving with her husband, Mark, to Arizona where he has continued to work as a radiologist in the southeast valley.

She has been a partner in the paractice since 2004. She handles complex medical problems of all ages. In addition, she performs well-woman care as she is uniquely qualified with her internal medicine qualifications.

Her two sons are Arizona natives and she spends a lot of her "free time" going to sporting and school events for her children.

Nurse Abigail Gerken

Abigail is originally from New Jersey where her initial career path was in teaching. She subsequently moved to Arizona and went to nursing school where she practiced as a nurse for 10 years before completing her nurse practitioner program.

In addition, she is undergoing additional training as a behavioral health practitioner -- an additional way that she can treat the "whole" person.
